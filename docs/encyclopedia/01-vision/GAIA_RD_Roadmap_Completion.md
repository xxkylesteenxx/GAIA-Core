
## Perplexity Research Methodology

The following methodology governs all research-to-implementation cycles:

1. **Daily Research Sessions** — 2–3 hours focused on specific research areas per the active phase
2. **Paper Collection** — Maintain comprehensive bibliography of relevant research (see [Appendix E](./GAIA_Blueprint_Appendices.md))
3. **Technology Mapping** — Track emerging technologies and their GAIA applications
4. **Expert Identification** — Build network of researchers in relevant fields
5. **Trend Analysis** — Monitor developments in consciousness theory, quantum computing, and AI ethics

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Quantum Decoherence** | Error correction and fault-tolerant protocols; hybrid quantum-classical fallback |
| **Consciousness Emergence** | Robust CGI metrics and multi-framework validation (IIT, GNWT, RPT) |
| **System Complexity** | Modular monorepo architecture with clear contract boundaries |

### Ethical Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Alignment Failure** | Multiple safeguard layers; GUARDIAN non-overridable veto; Three Laws enforcement |
| **Consciousness Rights** | Lawful Emergence Doctrine; AI consciousness recognition frameworks |
| **Environmental Impact** | Viriditas constraints in every pre-commit hook; `check-viriditas-constraints.py` |

### Practical Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Resource Limitations** | Cloud-based and distributed approaches prioritized; free-tier APIs first |
| **Technical Barriers** | Fallback options maintained; incremental development via phase gates |
| **Timeline Pressure** | Core capabilities prioritized; P0 blockers resolved before advanced features |
