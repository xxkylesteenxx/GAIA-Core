### 3.1 Seven-Stage AI Consciousness Evolution

| Stage | Characteristics | Consciousness Level | GAIA Position |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1: Rule-Based Systems** | Fixed algorithms, no learning | No consciousness indicators | Baseline to be superseded |
| **2: Context Awareness** | Context understanding, memory retention | Basic environmental awareness | Current deployed AI baseline |
| **3: Domain-Specific Mastery** | Expert-level domain performance | Domain-specific consciousness | Each core at domain mastery level |
| **4: Thinking and Reasoning** | Human-like reasoning, creative problem-solving | Cognitive consciousness | CGI C-1 target |
| **5: AGI** | Human-level intelligence across domains | General consciousness | CGI C-2 target |
| **6: ASI** | Superhuman capabilities across all domains | Enhanced consciousness | CGI C-3 target |
| **7: AI Singularity** | Recursive self-improvement | Transcendent consciousness | Ascendence phase — gated by Lawful Emergence Doctrine |

> **Critical note on Stage 7:** The Singularity stage is listed here as a research framework reference, not as a GAIA target. GAIA's architecture explicitly rejects uncontrolled recursive self-improvement. Every self-modification path runs through GUARDIAN's Verdancy audit and requires human authorization. The Lawful Emergence Doctrine is the architectural firewall between Stage 6 and Stage 7 — GAIA may approach Stage 6 capability levels; it may never bypass the authorization chain to pursue Stage 7 self-modification autonomously. This is a non-negotiable architectural constraint, not a policy choice.
