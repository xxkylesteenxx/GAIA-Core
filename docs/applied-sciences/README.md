# GAIA Applied Sciences & Engineering Series

This series documents the applied scientific and engineering disciplines that form the operational substrate of GAIA. Where the Formal Sciences series provides the logical and mathematical foundations, and the Natural Sciences series provides the empirical modeling layer, Applied Sciences & Engineering translates both into executable, deployable, auditable systems.

## Relationship to Other Series

- **Formal Sciences** → provides logic, mathematics, computation theory, and systems theory foundations
- **Natural Sciences** → provides physics, chemistry, biology, earth science, and neuroscience empirical models
- **Applied Sciences** → translates both into engineering decisions, implementation patterns, and operational constraints
- **Human Sciences** → provides social, psychological, and governance context for human-system interaction

## GAIA Integration

Every volume in this series concludes with explicit GAIA Design Implications and Research Priorities. These are not aspirational — they are binding architectural guidance that must be traceable to ADRs in `DECISIONS.md`.

## Canonical Principle

> Applied science without formal grounding is brittle. Formal science without applied translation is inert. GAIA requires both, integrated.
