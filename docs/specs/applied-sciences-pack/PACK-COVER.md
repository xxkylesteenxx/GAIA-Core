## Overview

This pack contains six new GAIA technical specifications requested for the next-stage buildout of the GAIA / ATLAS system stack:

1. **GAIA Applied Sciences Knowledge Graph Spec v1.0**
2. **GAIA Safety-Critical Systems Policy v1.0**
3. **GAIA Biomedical Interface and Biosafety Spec v1.0**
4. **GAIA Infrastructure Digital Twin Spec v1.0**
5. **GAIA Secure Compute and Verification Spec v1.0**
6. **GAIA Habitat and Food Systems Intelligence Spec v1.0**

These documents are written to be compatible with the existing GAIA governance and engineering corpus, especially:

- *GAIA Real-World Integration Standards v1.0*

## Design Intent

This pack extends GAIA from a broad research encyclopedia into a more operational specification layer. The documents therefore do four things simultaneously:

- define canonical technical scope;
- separate settled engineering practice from open research;
- express machine-checkable or policy-checkable requirements where possible;
- identify safety, governance, and verification gates for real-world deployment.

---

## Evidence Model

The specifications follow the same evidence ladder now used across the GAIA canonical corpus:

1. **Normative standard / regulation / official guidance**
2. **Institutional best practice**
3. **Peer-reviewed or field-validated engineering practice**
4. **GAIA design proposal**
5. **Open research hypothesis**

No open research hypothesis is to be treated as a production requirement without explicit upgrade through review, testing, and governance approval.

## Suggested Integration Order

1. Land the **Safety-Critical Systems Policy** and **Secure Compute and Verification Spec** first.
2. Then land the **Applied Sciences Knowledge Graph Spec** and **Infrastructure Digital Twin Spec**.
3. Then land the **Biomedical Interface and Biosafety Spec** and **Habitat and Food Systems Intelligence Spec**.
4. Finally, update the GAIA Governance Index and product-boundary documents to include these specs in the authority chain.
